Fanta is a fruit-flavored carbonated soft drink created by The Coca-Cola Company. It was first introduced in Germany in 1940 during World War II due to difficulties importing Coca-Cola syrup into the country. The name Fanta is derived from the German word Fantasie, meaning imagination, reflecting the creative process behind its development. Fanta has since become a popular beverage worldwide, available in various flavors and enjoyed by millions of people.
